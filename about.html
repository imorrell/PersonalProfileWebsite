About Me

Ivoire Morrel, 29, knows it was the transformative power of faith that
brought him back from the brink of destruction. After graduating from J.W.
Sexton High School in 2006, the Lansing, Mich., native enrolled at the
University of Michigan, where he majored in business marketing and pulled
down a 3.7 grade point average. But he withdrew after only one year and
spent the next several, he says, in “complete disarray.” In 2008, he enrolled
at Lansing Community College (LCC), where internal turmoil and lack of commitment
resulted in an abysmal 1.7 GPA. Morrell turned his life around in 2009 when he
became a born-again Christian, which “redirected me to a path of transcendence.”
Recommitted, he reenrolled at LCC in 2014, raised his GPA to 3.6 and graduated magna
cum laude with associate’s degrees in computer programming and business administration.
Now, at 29, he is a senior computer science major at Lawrence Technological University
(LTU) in Southfield, Mich. If it ended here, this story would indeed have a happy ending.
But the chapters between the bad and the good include more positive tales, including
Morrell’s time as Pre-College Initiative chair while at LTU, where he led efforts to
launch a NSBE Jr. chapter at the local Southfield Boys & Girls Club, and his founding
of Breakthrough United, a Christian organization for which he leads a Bible study class
and community outreach. Morrell now serves the city of Detroit as Microsoft’s Civic Tech
Fellow, using data analytics to help inform community organizers and decision makers
on how to solve problems ranging from crime to education in the Motor City.
